# Birds of a Feather Author Outreach

Build a strategic outreach network of complementary authors and thought leaders for any content project.

## Workflow Overview

1. **Analyze the content project** → Extract core themes, frameworks, and audience
2. **Identify "birds of a feather"** → Find authors with thematic overlap
3. **Research each author** → Current work, platforms, contact info
4. **Tier and prioritize** → Organize by strategic value
5. **Create outreach strategy** → Messaging angles and timing

## Phase 1: Content Analysis

Extract from the provided content:

**Core Themes** (3-5 main topics the work addresses)
- What problems does it solve?
- What transformation does it promise?
- What unique framework or methodology does it introduce?

**Target Audience**
- Who is the primary reader/viewer?
- What's their professional context?
- What stage of their journey are they in?

**Unique Positioning**
- What makes this different from existing work in the space?
- What's the author's credibility/background?
- What specific tools, frameworks, or concepts are introduced?

## Phase 2: Author Identification

Search for authors using this pattern:
- Books published 2020-present on overlapping themes
- Active platforms (podcasts, speaking circuits, courses)
- Engaged audiences (social following, community building)

**Search queries to use:**
```
"[theme] books 2024" OR "[theme] authors women"
"[topic] thought leader" OR "[topic] speaker"
"[framework concept] + book"
site:amazon.com "[theme]" books 2023 OR 2024 OR 2025
```

**Prioritize authors who:**
- Published recently (actively promoting = more likely to engage)
- Have platforms beyond just a book (podcasts, courses, communities)
- Share audience demographics with your content
- Have complementary rather than competing positioning

## Phase 3: Tiered Research

Organize authors into 4 tiers based on thematic alignment:

### Tier Structure

**Tier 1: Direct Theme Match** (highest priority)
- Core topic overlap with your work
- Same audience, same problems
- Potential for mutual endorsement or collaboration

**Tier 2: Methodology Alignment**
- Different topic but same approach/framework style
- Readers would naturally cross-pollinate

**Tier 3: Adjacent Expertise**
- Related field that complements your work
- Could provide credibility from adjacent domain

**Tier 4: Broader Category**
- Same general space, less direct overlap
- Useful for expanding network reach

### Research Each Author

For each author, gather:

1. **Why they matter**
   - Current role/platform
   - Credibility markers (awards, media, institutional affiliations)
   - Community size and engagement

2. **Thematic overlap**
   - 3-5 keywords connecting their work to yours
   - Specific concepts that align

3. **Platform presence**
   - Keynote speaking? Podcast? Courses?
   - Media appearances (Forbes, WSJ, podcasts)
   - Social media engagement level

4. **Contact information**
   - LinkedIn profile URL: `"author name" site:linkedin.com/in`
   - Website: `"author name" "book title" official website`
   - For dual-site authors, note both (personal + business)

5. **Book details**
   - Title, publisher, publication date
   - Key thesis/framework in one sentence

## Phase 4: Outreach Strategy

### LinkedIn Connection Messages

**Character limit: 300 characters** (LinkedIn connection request limit)

**Tone: Admirer-to-expert**
- Express genuine appreciation for their specific work
- Name a concrete concept or framework they created
- Position as peer exploring connection (not asking for anything yet)

**Message formula:**
```
Hi [Name] – [Specific thing you admire about their work]. I'm writing [your book/project] on [overlapping theme] and [brief connection point]. Would love to connect.
```

**Examples:**
```
Hi Jennifer – Your research on the seven hurdles women face advancing to leadership in "In Her Own Voice" really resonated. I'm writing on workplace confidence and self-advocacy. Would love to connect as peers in this space.
```

**DO NOT:**
- Ask for anything specific in first message
- Mention endorsements, quotes, or collaborations yet
- Use generic praise ("love your work")
- Exceed 300 characters

### Timing Opportunities

Flag authors with recent momentum:
- Book released in past 6 months (actively promoting)
- Award nomination or win
- Speaking tour announced
- Platform launch (podcast, course, community)
- Media appearance spike

These authors are building community NOW and more likely to engage.

## Phase 5: Deliverable Format

Produce a comprehensive document with:

### Section 1: Executive Summary
- Number of authors identified
- Tier breakdown
- Key timing opportunities

### Section 2: Full Author Profiles (by tier)
Each profile includes:
```markdown
### [Number]. [Author Name] – *[Book Title]* ([Year])

**Why they matter:** [2-3 sentences on platform and credibility]

**Thematic overlap:** [3-5 keyword themes]

**Platform:** [Speaking, podcast, courses, media]

**Contact:**
LinkedIn: [URL]
Website: [URL(s)]

**Book details:** [Title, publisher, date, one-sentence thesis]

**LinkedIn message:** [Ready-to-send message under 300 characters]
```

### Section 3: Research Summary
- Authors with dual-site strategies
- Authors using book/brand titles as domains
- Authors with academic/corporate presence only
- Authors missing LinkedIn (alternative contact strategies)

### Section 4: Outreach Timing & Strategy
- Who to contact first and why
- What to ask for (after connection established):
  - Quote/endorsement
  - Guest appearance on their platform
  - Cross-promotion
  - Speaking panel together
  - Interview exchange

## Quality Standards

**Verify all links** before including in final deliverable
- LinkedIn URLs should go to actual profiles
- Websites should show current copyright dates
- Note any broken or outdated links

**Distinguish between:**
- Confirmed contact (verified URL)
- Search placeholder (needs manual verification)
- No public presence found (suggest alternatives)

**For authors without LinkedIn:**
- Note alternative platforms (Instagram, Twitter)
- Suggest contact through co-authors
- Recommend publisher/agent contact forms
- Flag university/organization directories
