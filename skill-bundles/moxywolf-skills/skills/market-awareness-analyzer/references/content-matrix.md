# Content Strategy Matrix

## Stage 2 Dominant Market (>40% Problem-Aware)

**Situation:** Most of your market knows they have a problem but doesn't know solutions exist.

### Content Priorities
1. **Problem-validation content** (highest priority)
   - "Why [symptom] happens"
   - "The hidden cost of [problem]"
   - "Signs your [process] is broken"
   - "[X] mistakes that cause [consequence]"

2. **Educational content**
   - "What is [solution category]"
   - "Introduction to [approach]"
   - "How [solution type] works"

3. **Thought leadership**
   - Industry reports on the problem
   - Research on problem prevalence
   - Expert interviews about the pain

### What NOT to Create
- Product comparisons (they don't know products exist)
- Feature deep-dives (too early)
- Pricing content (not ready to buy)

### Forcing Function Strategy
Position to capture awareness when:
- Regulatory deadlines hit
- Industry incidents occur
- Competitors make noise

---

## Stage 3 Dominant Market (>40% Solution-Aware)

**Situation:** Most of your market is shopping for solutions but hasn't picked you.

### Content Priorities
1. **Category education** (highest priority)
   - "How to evaluate [solution category]"
   - "[Solution type] buyer's guide"
   - "What to look for in [solution]"
   - "[Category] comparison framework"

2. **Differentiation content**
   - "Why [your approach] vs [common approach]"
   - "[Your methodology] explained"
   - "The problem with [competitor approach]"

3. **Use case content**
   - "[Solution] for [specific industry]"
   - "How [company type] uses [solution category]"
   - "[Solution] for [specific role]"

### What NOT to Create
- Basic problem education (they already know)
- Generic "what is [problem]" content
- Overly broad industry content

### Competitive Strategy
- Direct comparison content is appropriate
- Feature matrices work at this stage
- Demo/trial offers are effective

---

## Stage 4 Dominant Market (>30% Product-Aware)

**Situation:** Most of your market knows about you specifically.

### Content Priorities
1. **Comparison content** (highest priority)
   - "[Your Product] vs [Competitor A]"
   - "[Your Product] vs [Competitor B]"
   - "Switching from [Competitor] to [Your Product]"

2. **Proof content**
   - Case studies with metrics
   - Customer testimonials
   - ROI calculators
   - Before/after analyses

3. **Conversion content**
   - Pricing transparency
   - Implementation guides
   - Onboarding previews
   - FAQ addressing objections

### What NOT to Create
- Educational content about the problem
- Category-level buyer's guides
- Awareness-stage thought leadership

### Conversion Strategy
- Focus on reducing friction
- Address specific objections from FAQ
- Provide social proof at decision points

---

## Evenly Distributed Market

**Situation:** Roughly equal distribution across stages.

### Content Strategy
Create stage-specific funnels:

```
Stage 2 Entry → Stage 3 Nurture → Stage 4 Conversion
     ↓                ↓                  ↓
Problem content → Category guides → Comparisons
     ↓                ↓                  ↓
   Email nurture → Demo offers → Sales conversation
```

### Content Calendar Approach
- 40% Stage 2 content (top of funnel)
- 40% Stage 3 content (middle of funnel)
- 20% Stage 4 content (bottom of funnel)

## External Forcing Functions

Always identify and plan around:

| Forcing Function | Content Response |
|------------------|------------------|
| Regulatory deadline | "How to comply by [date]" |
| Industry incident | "Lessons from [incident]" |
| Competitor news | "What [news] means for you" |
| Technology shift | "Preparing for [change]" |
| Economic pressure | "ROI of [solution] in [context]" |

Position content to capture awareness when forcing functions hit.
