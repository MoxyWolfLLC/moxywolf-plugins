# Podcast Booking Ladder

Build a five-phase podcast outreach strategy that realistically sequences bookings from accessible entry-level shows to aspirational top-tier platforms.

## Required Inputs

1. **Birds-of-a-feather output** (or equivalent research containing):
   - Book title, themes, and positioning
   - Author background and credibility markers
   - List of thematically-aligned authors with platforms
   - Target audience profile

2. **Market data spreadsheets** containing:
   - Domain names visited by target audiences
   - Affinity scores (higher = stronger audience alignment)
   - Traffic volumes
   - Source attribution (which author's audience)

## Workflow

### Step 1: Extract Author Assets & Gaps

From the birds-of-a-feather research, identify:

**Assets** (what makes them bookable):
- Media training or on-camera experience
- Unique framework or methodology
- Compelling origin story
- Institutional credibility
- Existing audience (even if small)

**Gaps** (what limits immediate access to top shows):
- No existing book sales numbers
- No podcast guest track record
- No viral moments or major press
- Limited social following
- Debut author status

This assessment determines Phase 1 starting point.

### Step 2: Analyze Market Data for Podcast Signals

From the spreadsheets, extract:

**Podcast platform usage:**
- Search for domains containing: podcast, pod, listen, spotify, apple, stitcher, podbean, anchor, buzzsprout, castbox, overcast, pocketcasts, iheart, tunein, audible
- Note which author audiences show highest podcast platform affinity

**Podcast discovery sites:**
- listennotes.com, podchaser.com, chartable.com, rephonic.com, goodpods.com, podnews.net
- High affinity to these = podcast-native audience

**Specific show domains:**
- Any domains that are podcast-specific (e.g., theinvestorspodcast.com)
- Cross-reference with birds-of-a-feather authors who host podcasts

**Media/publication domains:**
- Sites like fastcompany.com, inc.com, forbes.com with podcast arms
- HBR, McKinsey, and institutional platforms

### Step 3: Build the Five-Phase Ladder

Structure podcasts into phases based on REALISTIC accessibility:

#### Phase 1: Building the Reel (0-3 months pre-launch)
**Criteria for inclusion:**
- Solo hosts who need weekly/bi-weekly content
- Shows with <100 Apple reviews (hungry for guests)
- Interview format (not panel or co-host dependent)
- Thematic alignment with book topics
- No celebrity or bestseller guest requirements

**Goal:** 3-5 quality appearances to create clips and prove guest competence

#### Phase 2: Leveraging Early Coverage (Launch month)
**Criteria for inclusion:**
- Shows with 100-500 Apple reviews
- Hosts who book debut authors with early traction
- Larger audience but still accessible
- Can reference Phase 1 appearances in pitch

**Goal:** Credibility bump, "as heard on..." social proof

#### Phase 3: Established Mid-Tier Shows (Months 2-4 post-launch)
**Criteria for inclusion:**
- Shows with 500-2000 Apple reviews
- Require some proof of book performance
- May need warm introduction or publicist pitch
- Industry-recognized hosts

**Goal:** Significant audience reach, potential for viral clip

#### Phase 4: Birds-of-a-Feather Author Shows (Months 3-6 post-launch)
**Criteria for inclusion:**
- Podcasts hosted by authors from the birds-of-a-feather research
- Require established peer relationship or warm intro
- Thematic alignment already documented
- Often smaller but highly targeted audiences

**Goal:** Peer network building, endorsement pathway, cross-promotion

#### Phase 5: Top-Tier Aspirational (6+ months, requires breakout)
**Criteria for inclusion:**
- Major platform shows (millions of downloads)
- Require: bestseller status, significant press, or viral moment
- Often booked through agents/publicists only
- Celebrity or institutional hosts

**Goal:** Mass reach, mainstream credibility (may never happen—and that's okay)

### Step 4: Research Each Podcast

For each podcast in phases 1-4, gather:

**Podcast Profile:**
```
Podcast Name: [Name]
Host(s): [Name(s)]
Format: [Solo interview / Co-hosted / Panel]
Episode Cadence: [Weekly / Bi-weekly / Monthly]
Episode Count: [Approximate number]
Apple Rating/Reviews: [X.X / XXX reviews]
Audience Size Estimate: [Based on reviews, social, or stated downloads]
```

**Contact Information:**
```
Podcast URL: [Main website or hosting platform]
Host LinkedIn: [URL or "Not found - try [alternative]"]
Host Email: [If publicly available, or "Contact form at [URL]"]
Booking Process: [Direct pitch / Application form / Agent only / Warm intro required]
```

**Alignment Analysis:**
```
Thematic Overlap: [2-3 specific topic connections]
Audience Match: [Based on market data affinity scores]
Why Now: [Timing angle - recent episode topic, host's current focus, etc.]
```

### Step 5: Craft Outreach Messages

**Phase 1-2 Pitch Template (Direct, under 150 words):**
```
Subject: Guest pitch: [Specific angle] for [Podcast Name]

Hi [Host Name],

I'm [Author Name], [one-line credibility]. I'm writing because [specific recent episode] resonated with my work on [book topic].

My book [Title] explores [2-sentence hook]. I think your audience would connect with [specific angle for their show].

I'd love to share [concrete takeaway or framework] with your listeners.

[One-line on media experience if relevant]

Available for recording [timeframe]. Happy to send a one-sheet or chapter sample.

Best,
[Name]
```

**Phase 3-4 Pitch Template (Warmer, with proof points):**
```
Subject: [Mutual connection] suggested I reach out / Loved your episode on [topic]

Hi [Host Name],

[Opening that demonstrates familiarity with their work]

I recently appeared on [Phase 1-2 shows] discussing [topic], and [result/reception].

My book [Title] has [proof point: reviews, sales, press mention]. I think your audience would appreciate [specific angle].

[Connection point to their recent work or stated interests]

Would love to explore a conversation. I can send clips from previous appearances if helpful.

Best,
[Name]
```

**Phase 4 (Birds-of-a-Feather) Template:**
```
Subject: Fellow author in the [theme] space

Hi [Host Name],

We connected on LinkedIn [or: I've been following your work on [topic]]. Your [specific framework/concept] has influenced my thinking on [connection point].

My book [Title] is now out, exploring [brief description]. I'd love to come on [Podcast Name] to discuss [angle that complements rather than competes with their work].

I think there's a natural conversation between your [their concept] and my [your concept].

Would you be open to a guest appearance? Happy to discuss what would be most valuable for your audience.

Best,
[Name]
```

## Research Patterns

**Finding podcast contact info:**
```
"[podcast name]" guest pitch
"[podcast name]" booking
"[host name]" site:linkedin.com/in
"[podcast name]" contact email
"[host name]" email
```

**Estimating podcast size:**
- Apple Podcasts reviews as proxy (not perfect but accessible)
- Social following of host
- Stated download numbers (if disclosed)
- Guest caliber patterns

**Identifying booking process:**
- Check podcast website for "Be a Guest" or "Pitch" page
- Look for Calendly/booking links
- Note if they use a booking service (podmatch.com, matchmaker.fm)
- Identify if agent/publicist required

## Quality Standards

**For each podcast entry, verify:**
- [ ] Show is currently active (released episode in past 60 days)
- [ ] Host is still associated with show
- [ ] Contact method is current
- [ ] Thematic alignment is genuine (not forced)

**Realistic phase placement:**
- Phase 1 should be genuinely accessible (would say yes to a good pitch)
- Phase 5 should be honestly aspirational (may never happen)
- Don't inflate by placing accessible shows in later phases

**Contact information notes:**
- LinkedIn: Confirm profile belongs to correct person
- Email: Only include if publicly available (website, podcast description)
- If no direct contact: Note booking form URL or suggest alternative approach
