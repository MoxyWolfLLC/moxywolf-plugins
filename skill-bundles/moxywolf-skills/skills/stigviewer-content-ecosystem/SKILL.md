**When to use this skill:**

- Weekly blog content creation cycle
- When significant STIG or CMMC2 developments need narrative treatment
- To create integrated content campaigns from research through distribution

**Trigger phrases:**

- "Transform this research into the weekly blog post"

## Step 2: Create Narrative Blog Post

**Objective:** Transform research into compelling narrative blog using Sorkin DOB framework with MoxyWolf voice. Make stakes visceral, tension palpable, and readers feel the battle.

### Blog Post Structure

**Apply Sorkin DOB Framework:**
- **Desire**: What the character wants (win contracts, prove security, sustain trust)
- **Obstacle**: What stands in their way (regulatory complexity, scale problems, technical barriers)
- **Battle**: The conflict when desire meets obstacle (the struggle playing out in the ecosystem)

**Transform**: "Here's what changed" → "Here's what you're fighting for, what's blocking you, and the battle you're in"

### Narrative Structure

**Opening (Desire Established) - 2-3 paragraphs:**
- Show world before the shift
- What everyone wants (contracts, certifications, trust)
- End with emerging tension
- NO abstract overview, start visceral

❌ Avoid: "Defense contractors face increasing compliance requirements."
✅ Use: "Picture a CISO three weeks before audit, staring at 320 controls and a spreadsheet that hasn't updated since June. The POA&M they submitted six months ago? Already obsolete. The assessor arrives in 18 days."

**Rising Action (Obstacle Emerges) - 3-4 paragraphs:**
- Old methods stop working
- Stakes become urgent (specific dates, numbers)
- Show pressure building
- Use concrete examples from research

❌ Avoid: "Manual processes are challenging."
✅ Use: "You're managing 67 active STIGs. Each one updates quarterly. That's 268 changes per year across your infrastructure. Manual tracking stopped scaling somewhere around STIG number twelve."

**Battle Intensifies (Confrontation) - 4-5 paragraphs:**
- Ecosystem reorganizes itself
- New solutions emerge but fight isn't over
- Show both promise and remaining challenges
- Reference industry shifts, vendor responses
- Keep tension high

**Ending (Unresolved Tension) - 1-2 paragraphs:**
- DO NOT resolve cleanly
- Leave readers mid-battle with choice to make
- Frame ongoing struggle
- End with dramatic question

❌ Avoid: "Organizations that adopt automation will succeed."
✅ Use: "The question isn't whether automation wins. It's whether your organization figures that out before your competitor does – and before DoD makes the decision for you."

### Headline Formula

Capture DOB tension in headline:

❌ Avoid: "CMMC 2.0 Updates for 2026"
❌ Avoid: "How STIG Automation Helps CMMC Compliance"

✅ Use: "The Audit That Outgrew the Checklist: When Paperwork Stopped Being Enough"
✅ Use: "From Passing Audits to Proving Trust: The STIG Automation Reckoning"
✅ Use: "220,000 Contractors, 200 Assessors, One Deadline: Why Manual Evidence Just Died"

**Formula**: [Vivid situation] + [Stakes/tension] or [Old world] vs [New reality]

### MoxyWolf Voice Requirements (MANDATORY)

**Voice Elements:**
- Use contractions in 80%+ of opportunities ("doesn't," "can't," "it's")
- Vary sentence length aggressively (4-word fragments to 40-word buildups)
- Use fragments deliberately for emphasis
- Start sentences with And, But, Or
- Break topic-sentence patterns
- Replace abstract claims with specific, visceral details
- Always use typographer's quotes: "curly" not "straight"
- Use spaced en-dashes " – " sparingly, never em dashes (—)

**Forbidden Phrases** (AI tells - never use):
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It's important to understand..."
- "In today's rapidly changing..."
- "At its core..."
- "Moving forward..."
- "In order to..." (use "to")
- "Furthermore," "Additionally," "Moreover" (use And, or delete)

### Citation Format

Transform bracketed citations [1][2][3] into natural narrative authority.

❌ Avoid: "Articles argue automation is 'the only scalable path'.[11]"

✅ Use: "Fortra (2026) isn't being subtle about it: automation is 'the only scalable path' to CMMC Level 2. Manual evidence collection can't scale to 220,000 contractors racing toward the same certification deadline."

**Citation Format:**
Use (Author/Organization, Year) format integrated naturally into prose:
- "Steel Patriot Partners (2024) describes STIGs as..."
- "According to the Federal Register (2024), CMMC 2.0 requires..."
- "DoD's audit (Wiley Law, 2024) flagged weaknesses..."
- "Companies like Accorian (2024) and Integris IT (2024) have been blunt..."

**Technique:**
- Always include (Author/Organization, Year) for factual claims
- Weave citations into sentence structure naturally
- Place citation after the entity name or at end of claim
- Use direct quotes only when exact phrasing matters (keep under 15 words for copyright compliance)
- NO bracketed numbers in body text
- Include full source list at end with titles and URLs

### Content Requirements

**Tone & Voice:**
- **Visceral storytelling**: Put readers in scene with specific details
- **Data-driven**: Specific numbers, dates, sources woven into narrative
- **Authoritative**: Speak from 22 years compliance experience
- **Direct**: No marketing fluff, no AI tells
- **Urgent**: Timeline matters, convey pressure through stakes
- **Human-authentic**: Sounds natural when read aloud

**Technical Depth:**
- HIGH - audience expects technical accuracy
- Use proper terminology (STIGs, NIST 800-53, 800-171, CMMC Level 2)
- Explain acronyms on first use
- Include technical details that matter (update cycles, control counts)
- Respect audience intelligence with precision

**Narrative vs. Report:**
- Tell a story, don't write a report
- Show tension building through piece
- Use second-person or specific scenarios
- Create empathy through concrete examples
- Leave readers mid-battle, don't resolve cleanly

**Data & Citations:**
- Every claim backed by source
- Specific numbers (not "many" but "338,000")
- Exact dates (not "recently" but "November 10, 2025")
- Use (Author/Organization, Year) format
- NO bracketed citation numbers in body
- Quotes under 15 words (copyright compliance)
- Full source list with titles and URLs at end

**Length:** 1,200-1,800 words (8-12 minute read)

**Formatting:**
- Thematic section breaks (not "Act I, Act II")
- Short paragraphs (3-5 sentences)
- Vary paragraph length (include single-sentence paragraphs)
- Bold key statistics sparingly
- No emoji
- Typographer's quotes throughout
- Spaced en-dashes " – " only, never em dashes

**Anti-AI Checklist** (Run before completion):
- [ ] Contractions used in 80%+ of opportunities
- [ ] No forbidden phrases (It's worth noting, At its core, etc.)
- [ ] No formulaic transitions (Furthermore, Additionally, Moreover)
- [ ] Sentence length varies dramatically (4-word and 40-word sentences present)
- [ ] At least one fragment per section
- [ ] Parallelism broken (not everything in threes)
- [ ] Specific examples replace abstract claims
- [ ] Paragraphs vary in length
- [ ] Sentences start with And, But, or Or in each section

- [ ] Could be read aloud and sound natural

**SEO Considerations:**

- Include in headline, opening, section breaks naturally

## Step 4: Generate Bibliography with Abstracts

**Objective:** Create a BibTeX bibliography file with substantive abstracts for every source cited in the blog post.

### Bibliography Requirements

**BibTeX Entry Format:**
```bibtex
@misc{authorOrg2025,
  author = {Author Name or Organization},
  title = {Full Title of Source},
  year = {2025},
  url = {https://full-url.com},
  note = {Accessed: 2025-01-08},
  abstract = {2-4 sentence abstract describing the main contribution, specific findings, and relevance to STIG/CMMC2 convergence.}
}
```

**Abstract Quality Standards:**

**Length:** 2-4 sentences (50-150 words)

**Content Must Include:**
1. **Main Argument or Contribution:** What does this source claim or provide?
2. **Specific Findings or Data:** Actual numbers, dates, or concrete findings
3. **Methodology or Context:** How was this determined or what's the background?
4. **Relevance to Topic:** Why this matters for STIG/CMMC2 compliance

**Good Abstract Example:**
```
This DoD memorandum announces Phase 1 enforcement of CMMC 2.0 
beginning November 10, 2025, affecting approximately 338,000 defense 
industrial base contractors, of which 68% are small businesses. 
The policy requires CMMC Level 1 or Level 2 certification prior to 
contract award, with Phase 2 mandatory third-party assessments 
beginning November 10, 2026. This represents the culmination of a 
multi-year DoD effort to strengthen supply chain cybersecurity.
```

**Bad Abstract Example:**
```
This article discusses CMMC and its importance for defense contractors.
```
*(Too vague, no specifics, no data, no clear contribution)*

### Abstract Writing Process

**For Each Source:**

1. **Identify Main Contribution:**
   - What's the one thing this source adds to understanding?
   - Is it data, policy, analysis, or technical guidance?

2. **Extract Specific Data:**
   - Numbers: costs, timelines, counts, percentages
   - Dates: enforcement deadlines, release schedules
   - Technical details: control counts, framework relationships
   - Quotes: key policy language or authoritative statements

3. **Explain Methodology:**
   - Official announcement vs. industry analysis
   - Survey data vs. policy document
   - Vendor perspective vs. regulatory guidance

4. **State Relevance:**
   - How does this connect STIG and CMMC2?
   - Why does this matter for compliance professionals?
   - What decision does this inform?

### Source Categories & Citation Keys

**Official Government Sources:**
- Citation key format: `disa2025` or `dod2025cmmc`
- Entry type: `@techreport` or `@misc`
- Abstract focus: Policy details, official timelines, requirements

**Vendor/Tool Sources:**
- Citation key format: `steelcloud2025` or `puppet2025automation`
- Entry type: `@misc` or `@article`
- Abstract focus: Technical capabilities, ROI data, implementation details

**Industry Analysis:**
- Citation key format: `gartner2025` or `forrester2025compliance`
- Entry type: `@article` or `@techreport`
- Abstract focus: Market trends, cost analysis, strategic implications

**News Sources:**
- Citation key format: `fedscoop2025` or `defenseone2025`
- Entry type: `@article`
- Abstract focus: Recent developments, contractor reactions, expert quotes

### Bibliography File Format

**Filename:** `stigviewer_weekly_[date]_bibliography.bib`

**Structure:**
- Alphabetical by citation key
- Proper BibTeX syntax
- URLs on single line (no line breaks)
- Abstracts in proper prose (not bullet points)
- Consistent formatting

## Step 5: Craft LinkedIn Post

**Objective:** Write a single high-impact LinkedIn post that drives traffic to the blog with a clear CTA.

### LinkedIn Post Structure

**Format:**
```markdown
[HOOK: 1-2 sentences, must work standalone]

[DEVELOPMENT: 2-3 paragraphs building the insight]

[DATA: 2-3 specific statistics or findings]

[STRATEGIC INSIGHT: The reframe or competitive angle]

[CTA TO BLOG]

#Hashtag1 #Hashtag2 #Hashtag3 #Hashtag4
```

### Writing Guidelines

**Hook Requirements:**
- First 2 lines (before "see more") must create intrigue
- Start with specific date, number, or surprising fact
- Pose tension or contradiction
- Examples:
  - "Three months ago, CMMC 2.0 was theory. Today, it's affecting 338,000 defense contractors."
  - "Most DIB contractors are looking in the wrong place for CMMC compliance."
  - "November 10, 2025 changed everything for defense contractors. Here's what they're missing."

**Body Content:**
- **Length:** 1,300-1,500 characters (sweet spot for LinkedIn algorithm)
- **Paragraphs:** 2-3 sentences max, with line breaks between
- **Data:** Include 2-3 specific statistics with context
- **Voice:** Authoritative but conversational, like colleague sharing insight
- **Formatting:**
  - Line breaks every 2-3 sentences
  - Use → or • for bullet points (sparingly)
  - Bold key numbers or concepts (but not excessively)
  - Emoji only if truly appropriate (rare for technical content)

**Strategic Content:**
- Don't just summarize blog
- Lead with the most surprising insight
- Use the "hidden in plain sight" angle
- Create FOMO around competitive advantage
- Position blog as deeper analysis

**CTA Strategy:**
- **Direct approach:** "Read the full analysis on the STIGViewer blog: [LINK]"
- **Value-first:** "I've broken down the complete timeline and cost analysis: [LINK]"
- **Question-based:** "Want to know which 10 STIGs matter most? Full details here: [LINK]"

**Always mention lead magnet:**
"Also, I've created a 90-day implementation checklist you can download [describe brief benefit]"

### Hashtag Strategy

**Count:** 4-6 hashtags (optimal for reach without spam)

**Categories:**
1. **Primary Topic:** #CMMC2 #STIGs #DefenseCompliance
2. **Audience Role:** #CISO #ComplianceProfessional #CyberSecurity
3. **Outcome:** #RiskManagement #SecurityAutomation #AuditReadiness
4. **Industry:** #DefenseIndustrial #DIB #GovernmentContracting

**Placement:** End of post, after CTA

**Example:** `#CMMC2 #DefenseCompliance #CISO #SecurityAutomation`
