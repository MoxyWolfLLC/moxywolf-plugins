# LinkedIn Post Examples

This reference provides complete examples of high-converting LinkedIn posts across three distinct tones.

## Understanding the Three Variants

### Variant 1: Direct/Urgent
- **Audience:** Decision-makers who respond to deadlines and data
- **Hook:** Recent event, enforcement date, or breaking change
- **Emotion:** Urgency, FOMO, competitive pressure
- **Best for:** Compliance, regulatory, time-sensitive topics

### Variant 2: Insight/Strategy
- **Audience:** Strategic thinkers who value frameworks and patterns
- **Hook:** Contrarian insight or hidden opportunity
- **Emotion:** Intellectual curiosity, strategic advantage
- **Best for:** Thought leadership, methodology, industry analysis

### Variant 3: Personal/Narrative
- **Audience:** Relationship-focused professionals who connect with stories
- **Hook:** Personal experience or observation
- **Emotion:** Trust, authenticity, shared experience
- **Best for:** Founder positioning, expertise demonstration, community building

## Formatting Best Practices

### Visual Structure
- **Line breaks** - Every 2-3 sentences for readability
- **Bullets** - Use → or • for scannable lists
- **Sections** - Bold headers to break up content
- **Emojis** - 2-3 max, only for CTAs or emphasis (📊 👉 📥)

### Length Guidelines
- **Optimal:** 1,300-1,500 characters (LinkedIn's algorithm sweet spot)
- **Hook:** First 2 lines must work standalone (preview text)
- **CTA placement:** Final 3-4 lines with clear links

### Voice Calibration

**Direct/Urgent Voice:**
- Short sentences
- Data-heavy (numbers, percentages, dates)
- Action verbs (affecting, requiring, eliminating)
- Competitive framing

**Insight/Strategy Voice:**
- Medium sentences
- Framework-oriented (patterns, systems, principles)
- Comparison structures (❌ vs ✅)
- Educational tone

**Personal/Narrative Voice:**
- Variable sentence length for rhythm
- "I" statements and personal observations
- Past tense for credibility ("I've seen...")
- Community-building language

## Common Mistakes

❌ **Opening with question** - "Have you heard about CMMC 2.0?"
✅ **Opening with statement** - "CMMC 2.0 just became mandatory."

❌ **Burying the CTA** - Links in middle of post
✅ **Clear final CTA** - Dedicated section with both links

❌ **Too much promotion** - 80% self-promotion, 20% value
✅ **Value-first** - 80% insights/data, 20% promotion

❌ **Vague promises** - "Learn how to improve your compliance"
✅ **Specific outcomes** - "90-day implementation roadmap with cost estimates"

❌ **Wall of text** - No line breaks or formatting
✅ **Scannable** - Line breaks every 2-3 sentences, bullets, bold
