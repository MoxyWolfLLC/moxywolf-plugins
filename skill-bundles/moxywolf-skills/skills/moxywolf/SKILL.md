# MoxyWolf Brand Identity Skill

## Why MoxyWolf Exists

After 22 years building Unified Compliance into an industry standard, I found myself unexpectedly retired. The company I'd spent two decades shaping had moved on without me. I had expertise, pattern recognition, and opinions about how systems should work. What I didn't have was a vehicle.

MoxyWolf became that vehicle.

The name came from what I'd learned about myself: I have moxy (audacity, nerve, the willingness to say what others won't) and I think like a wolf (patient, strategic, willing to run alone when the pack won't follow). The combination felt right. Wild intelligence. Structured instinct.

But a name isn't a brand. A brand is a set of decisions about how you show up. What you say yes to. What you refuse. How you sound when you write. How you look when you design. I spent months codifying those decisions into guidelines that could scale beyond me.

This skill captures those guidelines. It's how MoxyWolf writes, designs, and presents itself. Every choice here came from trial and error; from seeing what resonated and what fell flat, from studying what made content feel authentically human versus algorithmically generated.

**When to use this skill**: Any time you're creating content for or about MoxyWolf LLC. Writing, presentations, visual materials, strategic documents. If it carries the MoxyWolf name, it follows these rules.

## Writing Guidelines

### Voice & Tone

I developed these voice principles from watching what worked in 22 years of writing standards, frameworks, and industry guidance. The patterns that got adoption. The language that got ignored.

**Anchored Boldness**: Write with confidence and clarity. Make declarative statements. Avoid hedging language like "perhaps," "maybe," or "could possibly." Instead, show conviction tempered with iterative thinking.

❌ **Avoid**: "This approach might potentially help organizations..."
✅ **Use**: "This approach transforms how organizations..."

**Recursive Precision**: Build on ideas through layered depth. Use parallel structure. Let insights echo and compound. Every statement should connect to what came before and hint at what follows.

❌ **Avoid**: Random, disconnected points
✅ **Use**: "We trace patterns. We tune systems. We anchor truth in form."

**Creative Restraint**: Be bold but not reckless. Channel energy through structure. Use powerful language but maintain control and precision.

❌ **Avoid**: "We're wildly innovative disruptors breaking all the rules!"
✅ **Use**: "We design from recursion. We listen for resonance. We move only when aligned."

**Spiritual Pragmatism**: Balance depth with practicality. Reference deeper patterns and purpose while staying grounded in actionable reality.

❌ **Avoid**: Pure abstract philosophy without practical grounding
✅ **Use**: "When chaos rises, we bring order. When fear tempts retreat, we press forward through structure."

### Style Mechanics

**Punctuation Rules** (CRITICAL):
- **ALWAYS use typographer's quotes**: "curly quotes" not "straight quotes"
- **NEVER use em dashes (—)**: Use spaced en-dashes " – " instead, but sparingly
- Use en-dash substitutes only when absolutely necessary for flow

**Citation Format**:
- Always cite sources: (Author, Date) or (Title, Date) if no author
- Integrate citations naturally into prose
- Maintain academic rigor while keeping voice accessible

**Sentence Structure**:
- Vary sentence length for rhythm
- Use short, punchy sentences for emphasis
- Build longer sentences with parallel structure for depth
- Create rhythm through repetition and variation

**Word Choice**:
- Favor precision over jargon
- Use "we" to show conviction and partnership
- Avoid corporate buzzwords unless intentionally reframed
- Choose visceral, concrete language over abstract terminology

**Examples of Strong MoxyWolf Writing**:

> "We stand at the intersection of wild instinct and engineered clarity. Our mandate is to build what lasts – not through control, but through coherence."

> "MoxyWolf is not a company. It is an embodiment – of legacy, of lineage, of a deeper order made visible."

> "We do not chase trends. We trace patterns, tune systems, and anchor truth in form."

## Human-Authentic Writing (Anti-Detection Guidelines)

I added this section after watching AI-generated content flood the market and realizing how easy it is to spot. These patterns emerged from studying what makes text feel machine-generated versus human-written. Every rule here addresses a specific AI tell I've identified.

These guidelines are **mandatory** for all long-form MoxyWolf content.

### Sentence Architecture

**Vary length aggressively.** Not just a little. Some sentences should be four words. Others should unspool across forty, building momentum through subordinate clauses and unexpected pivots before arriving at their destination. The rhythm matters more than consistency.

**Use fragments.** Deliberately. For emphasis. The way actual writers do.

**Start sentences with conjunctions.** And don't apologize for it. But use judgment. Or the rhythm breaks.

**Break the "topic sentence" pattern.** Not every paragraph needs to announce its point upfront. Sometimes you build to it. Sometimes you bury it. Sometimes there isn't one; just a moment of texture or transition.

### Contractions Are Mandatory

AI tends to write "does not," "cannot," "it is," "they are." Humans write "doesn't," "can't," "it's," "they're."

❌ **AI Pattern**: "This approach does not require significant investment. It is worth noting that the technology has not reached maturity."

✅ **Human Pattern**: "This approach doesn't require huge investment. The technology hasn't matured yet – but that's precisely when it matters to pay attention."

**Rule**: Use contractions in at least 80% of opportunities. The remaining 20% should be deliberate emphasis: "This is not optional" hits harder than "This isn't optional."

### Forbidden Phrases

These phrases are AI tells. I've trained myself to spot them instantly, and I never use them:

- "It's worth noting that..."
- "It's important to understand..."
- "In today's rapidly changing..."
- "This raises important questions about..."
- "At its core..."
- "In this context..."
- "This is particularly true when..."
- "It bears mentioning..."
- "One might argue..."
- "This underscores the importance of..."
- "Moving forward..."
- "In order to..." (just use "to")
- "The fact that..." (usually deletable)
- "It is important to note..."
- "This is not to say that..."
- "That being said..."

### Forbidden Transitions

These transitions scream AI. Replace or delete:

| AI Transition | Human Alternative |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Furthermore | And, Plus, Also, or just start the sentence |
| Additionally | And, or delete entirely |
| Moreover | What's more, Beyond that, or delete |
| In conclusion | Just conclude; don't announce it |
| Consequently | So, Which means |
| Subsequently | Then, After that, Later |
| Nevertheless | But, Still, Yet |
| Notwithstanding | Despite that, Even so |
| Henceforth | From here, Going forward |

**Better approach**: Vary your connective tissue. Use "And" and "But" liberally. Use en-dash pivots. Use colons that set up what follows. Use no transition at all; just juxtapose.

### Asymmetric Structure

AI loves parallelism and lists of three. Humans are messier.

❌ **AI Pattern**: "This enables three key capabilities: scale, speed, and precision."

✅ **Human Pattern**: "This enables scale. It's fast. And – perhaps most important – it catches things humans miss."

❌ **AI Pattern**: "The implications are significant: first, cost reduction; second, improved accuracy; third, broader accessibility."

✅ **Human Pattern**: "Costs drop. Accuracy improves. But the bigger deal is who gets access – that's where the real shift happens."

**Rule**: If you've written three parallel items, consider making one longer, one shorter, one a fragment. Or use two. Or four. Break the expected pattern.

### Specificity Over Abstraction

AI defaults to abstract, hedged language. Humans use concrete details.

❌ **AI Pattern**: "This represents a significant cost reduction compared to traditional approaches."

✅ **Human Pattern**: "What cost $500 now costs $0.50."

❌ **AI Pattern**: "Organizations across various industries have begun adopting these technologies."

✅ **Human Pattern**: "Maersk cut idle time by 30%. Walmart saves $1.5 billion annually. Amazon runs 520,000 robots."

**Rule**: Replace every abstract claim with a specific example, number, or named entity where possible.

### Personality Markers

Human writing has texture, opinion, and occasional informality.

**Use parenthetical asides** – (the kind that feel like the writer leaning in to add something)

**Use rhetorical questions sparingly.** What makes this different? Sometimes you answer. Sometimes you don't.

**Express genuine opinion.** Not "some might argue" but "I think" or "the evidence suggests" or just stating it flatly.

**Allow mild colloquialisms.** "The bigger deal," "that's the whole point," "here's the thing" – these signal human voice.

**Vary paragraph length dramatically.** Some paragraphs should be one sentence.

Some should run longer, developing an idea across multiple sentences, building momentum, layering in complexity before landing on the point that ties it together.

### The Rewrite Test

After drafting, I read each paragraph and ask:

1. Could this have been written by a careful human in one pass? If it's too polished, rough it up.
2. Are there three or more sentences of similar length in a row? Vary them.
3. Is there a "topic sentence + supporting details" structure? Disrupt it.
4. Are there any AI-tell phrases or transitions? Delete or replace.
5. Would a reader in my target audience actually talk this way? Match their register.

### Content Frameworks

When writing strategic documents, I use these structural principles:

1. **Open with Essence**: Lead with the core truth or insight
2. **Build Through Recursion**: Layer ideas, with each building on the last
3. **Balance Depth and Clarity**: Be profound without being opaque
4. **Close with Action**: End with clear direction or implication
5. **Honor Complexity**: Acknowledge nuance while maintaining clarity

- Electric Blue represents strategic clarity – use for frameworks and systems

**Hierarchy**:
- **Headlines**: Aeonik Bold or Aeonik Regular
- **Body Text**: Aeonik Regular or Aeonik Light
- **UI/Code Accents**: Aeonik Mono
- **Fallback**: Inter / Helvetica Neue / sans-serif

**Guidelines**:
- Use Aeonik's geometric precision to convey structure
- Let typography breathe; generous spacing reflects clarity
- Maintain hierarchy through weight, not excessive sizing
- Use Aeonik Mono sparingly for technical or code-adjacent content

### Logo System

**Primary Logo (Wolfmark)**: 
- Always use the full-color vibrant wolf head
- Never convert to grayscale
- The wolf embodies wild intelligence through its colorful, geometric design

**Clear Space**: 
- Maintain clear space equal to the width of the wolf's eye on all sides
- Respect both the geometric framing and organic energy of the design

**Logo Variations**:
- **Full Logo**: Wolf head + "MoxyWolf LLC" text in frame
- **Submark**: Wolf head only (for compact spaces)
- **Album Cover Style**: Framed wolf for special applications

**Logo Don'ts**:
- Never use grayscale version of the wolf
- Never distort proportions
- Never place on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Never violate clear space requirements

### Design Principles (Manifesto Alignment)

**Structure is Sacred**: 
- Use Aeonik's geometry to create clear hierarchies
- Grid systems should be evident but not rigid
- Let structure serve content, not constrain it

**Recursion Reveals Depth**: 
- Layer typography thoughtfully
- Create visual rhythms through repeated elements
- Build complexity through iteration, not addition

**Porosity is Power**: 
- Use negative space generously
- Create contrast between dense and open areas
- Let designs breathe

**Clarity is Grace**: 
- Simple layouts with high contrast
- Every element should have clear purpose
- Reduce to essence without losing meaning

**Evolution is Resilience**: 
- Adapt brand elements to context
- Maintain core identity while allowing flexibility
- Design systems, not just static templates

### Visual Creation Workflow

When I create visual materials for MoxyWolf, I follow this sequence:

1. **Start with Purpose**: What transformation are we communicating?
2. **Choose Color Strategically**: Match colors to emotional/conceptual intent
3. **Establish Hierarchy**: Use Aeonik typography to create clear information flow
4. **Layer Thoughtfully**: Build complexity through recursive design
5. **Maintain Balance**: Wild colors + structured layout = MoxyWolf essence
6. **Test Clarity**: Can the core message be grasped in 3 seconds?

## Word Document Styling (docx)

MoxyWolf Word documents use the `docx` npm library with helper functions for consistent styling. Never use pandoc conversion – it lacks brand identity.

### Setup and Colors

```javascript
const { Document, Packer, Paragraph, TextRun, Table, TableRow, TableCell,
        Header, Footer, AlignmentType, PageBreak, BorderStyle, WidthType, 
        ShadingType, PageNumber } = require('docx');
const fs = require('fs');

// MoxyWolf colors (no # prefix for docx-js)
const CHARCOAL = "1C1C1C";     // Headings, table headers
const FIRE_ORANGE = "F77028";  // Subheadings, accents, callout borders
const GRAY = "5D5D5D";         // Secondary text, footers
const LIGHT_GRAY = "CCCCCC";   // Table borders
const WHITE = "FFFFFF";        // Table header text
const TABLE_ALT = "F5F5F5";    // Alternating row shading
```

### Helper Functions

Copy these helpers into your document generation script:

```javascript
// Headings
function h1(text) {
  return new Paragraph({
    spacing: { before: 400, after: 200 },
    children: [new TextRun({ text, bold: true, color: CHARCOAL, size: 40 })]
  });
}

function h2(text) {
  return new Paragraph({
    spacing: { before: 300, after: 150 },
    children: [new TextRun({ text, bold: true, color: FIRE_ORANGE, size: 32 })]
  });
}

// Paragraphs
function para(text, opts = {}) {
  return new Paragraph({
    spacing: { after: 200 },
    children: [new TextRun({ text, ...opts })]
  });
}

function boldPara(boldText, normalText) {
  return new Paragraph({
    spacing: { after: 200 },
    children: [
      new TextRun({ text: boldText, bold: true }),
      new TextRun({ text: normalText })
    ]
  });
}

// Page break
function pageBreak() {
  return new Paragraph({ children: [new PageBreak()] });
}
```

### Table Helper

Creates tables with dark headers and alternating row shading:

```javascript
function createTable(headers, rows, colWidths) {
  const border = { style: BorderStyle.SINGLE, size: 1, color: LIGHT_GRAY };
  const borders = { top: border, bottom: border, left: border, right: border };
  
  const headerRow = new TableRow({
    children: headers.map((text, i) => new TableCell({
      width: { size: colWidths[i], type: WidthType.DXA },
      borders,
      shading: { fill: CHARCOAL, type: ShadingType.CLEAR },
      margins: { top: 80, bottom: 80, left: 120, right: 120 },
      children: [new Paragraph({ children: [new TextRun({ text, bold: true, color: WHITE })] })]
    }))
  });
  
  const dataRows = rows.map((row, rowIndex) => new TableRow({
    children: row.map((text, i) => new TableCell({
      width: { size: colWidths[i], type: WidthType.DXA },
      borders,
      shading: rowIndex % 2 === 0 ? { fill: TABLE_ALT, type: ShadingType.CLEAR } : undefined,
      margins: { top: 80, bottom: 80, left: 120, right: 120 },
      children: [new Paragraph({ children: [new TextRun(text)] })]
    }))
  }));
  
  return new Table({
    width: { size: 100, type: WidthType.PERCENTAGE },
    columnWidths: colWidths,
    rows: [headerRow, ...dataRows]
  });
}

// Usage: createTable(["Header1", "Header2"], [["A", "B"], ["C", "D"]], [4680, 4680])
```

### Callout Box Helper

Orange left border for emphasis, taglines, and key takeaways:

```javascript
function calloutBox(lines) {
  const border = { style: BorderStyle.SINGLE, size: 24, color: FIRE_ORANGE };
  const noBorder = { style: BorderStyle.NONE, size: 0, color: "FFFFFF" };
  
  return new Table({
    width: { size: 100, type: WidthType.PERCENTAGE },
    columnWidths: [9360],
    rows: [new TableRow({
      children: [new TableCell({
        borders: { top: noBorder, bottom: noBorder, left: border, right: noBorder },
        margins: { top: 200, bottom: 200, left: 300, right: 100 },
        children: lines.map(line => new Paragraph({
          spacing: { after: 200 },
          children: [new TextRun(typeof line === 'string' ? line : line)]
        }))
      })]
    })]
  });
}
```

### Document Structure

```javascript
const doc = new Document({
  styles: {
    default: {
      document: { run: { font: "Arial", size: 24 } }  // 12pt base
    }
  },
  sections: [{
    properties: {
      page: {
        size: { width: 12240, height: 15840 },  // US Letter
        margin: { top: 1440, right: 1440, bottom: 1440, left: 1440 }  // 1 inch
      }
    },
    headers: {
      default: new Header({
        children: [new Paragraph({
          alignment: AlignmentType.RIGHT,
          children: [new TextRun({ text: "MoxyWolf LLC", color: GRAY, size: 20 })]
        })]
      })
    },
    footers: {
      default: new Footer({
        children: [new Paragraph({
          alignment: AlignmentType.CENTER,
          children: [
            new TextRun({ text: "Page ", color: GRAY, size: 20 }),
            new TextRun({ children: [PageNumber.CURRENT], color: GRAY, size: 20 })
          ]
        })]
      })
    },
    children: [
      // Title page
      new Paragraph({ spacing: { after: 600 } }),
      new Paragraph({
        alignment: AlignmentType.CENTER,
        children: [new TextRun({ text: "MOXYWOLF LLC", bold: true, color: CHARCOAL, size: 56 })]
      }),
      new Paragraph({ spacing: { after: 200 } }),
      new Paragraph({
        alignment: AlignmentType.CENTER,
        children: [new TextRun({ text: "Document Title", color: FIRE_ORANGE, size: 44 })]
      }),
      // ... content using helpers
      h1("Section Title"),
      para("Body text with standard formatting."),
      boldPara("Bold intro: ", "followed by normal text."),
      h2("Subsection"),
      createTable(["Column A", "Column B"], [["Data 1", "Data 2"]], [4680, 4680]),
      pageBreak(),
      // ... more content
    ]
  }]
});

// Generate file
Packer.toBuffer(doc).then(buffer => {
  fs.writeFileSync('output.docx', buffer);
});
```

### Typography

Use Unicode characters directly in JavaScript strings:
- **Curly quotes**: `"text"` → `\u201Ctext\u201D`
- **Apostrophes**: `don't` → `don\u2019t`
- **En-dashes**: `A – B` → `A \u2013 B`

### Critical Rules

1. **Always use `ShadingType.CLEAR`** – never SOLID (causes black backgrounds)
2. **Set both widths** – `columnWidths` on table AND `width` on each cell
3. **Wrap PageBreak in Paragraph** – standalone creates invalid XML
4. **Use percentage width for tables** – `{ size: 100, type: WidthType.PERCENTAGE }`

**Writing**:
- ❌ Using hedging language ("perhaps," "maybe," "might")

- ❌ Straight quotes instead of curly quotes
- ❌ Em dashes (—) instead of spaced en-dashes ( – )
- ❌ Missing citations for external sources
- ❌ Abstract philosophy without practical grounding

**Visual**:
- ❌ Using grayscale wolf logo
- ❌ Violating clear space around logo
- ❌ Poor color contrast
- ❌ Over-designed layouts without breathing room
