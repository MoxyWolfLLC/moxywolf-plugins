# Sorkin DOB Narrative Framework

Aaron Sorkin's Desire-Obstacle-Battle framework adapted for nonfiction, technical writing, and structured content.

## The Three Elements

### Desire
What the reader viscerally wants. Not what they "should" want — what keeps them up at night.

- Draw from Phase 1 intent answers (thesis, reader profile, stakes)
- Make it specific and emotional, not abstract
- The Desire must be something the reader feels BEFORE encountering the work

**Good**: "The reader wants to pass their CMMC audit without losing six months and half their engineering team to documentation theater."
**Bad**: "The reader wants to understand compliance frameworks."

### Obstacle
What stands between the reader and their Desire. The force, system, conventional wisdom, or adversary that makes the Desire hard to achieve.

- Must be real and honestly presented — never strawmanned
- The reader should feel the weight of the Obstacle, understand why it persists
- Can be systemic (industry practices), intellectual (wrong mental model), adversarial (opposing framework), or structural (organizational inertia)

**Good**: "Greene's Law 3 — 'Conceal Your Intentions' — works because most compliance environments reward opacity. The person who hides their gaps until the auditor leaves gets promoted. The person who surfaces problems gets blamed for them."
**Bad**: "Some people don't follow best practices."

### Battle
How the chapter's argument fights through the Obstacle. Not a clean victory — a struggle that the reader can follow and learn from.

- The Battle uses the author's specific experience, examples, and counter-arguments
- It should feel earned, not proclaimed
- Leave some tension unresolved — the reader finishes mid-battle with a decision to make

**Good**: "Here's what happened when Jody's team at [company] surfaced every gap on day one of the audit prep. The auditor's reaction. The board's reaction. The six-week timeline that competitors spent six months on."
**Bad**: "The solution is to be transparent. Transparency leads to better outcomes."

## DOB at Different Scales

### Book-Level Throughline
For multi-chapter works, map a macro DOB that spans the entire work:
- **Desire**: The reader's overarching want (set in Phase 1)
- **Obstacle**: The systemic challenge the entire book addresses
- **Battle**: The journey through chapters — each chapter advances the battle

The book-level DOB does NOT resolve in any single chapter. Each chapter contributes to the larger arc.

### Chapter-Level DOB
Every chapter gets its own complete DOB arc:
- **Desire**: What the reader wants by the end of THIS chapter
- **Obstacle**: The specific barrier or opposing force for this chapter's topic
- **Battle**: How this chapter's argument fights through

Chapter DOB arcs should connect to the book-level throughline but stand on their own.

### Counter-Playbook DOB Pattern
When chapters have an adversarial structure (the work argues against an opposing framework):

- **Desire**: The reader wants to succeed with integrity — to win without becoming the person the opposing playbook describes
- **Obstacle**: The opposing rule/law/principle — presented honestly, showing WHY it works and why it's tempting. Do not strawman. The reader needs to feel the pull.
- **Battle**: The counter-rule as a structural counter-move. Not moral superiority — strategic advantage. Show how the ethical path wins through better mechanics, not better intentions.

**Example for The Assured Playbook vs. Greene's 48 Laws:**
- **Desire**: "I want to build a compliant organization that wins contracts without playing political games"
- **Obstacle**: Greene's Law 7 — "Get Others to Do the Work for You, but Always Take the Credit." This works because compliance teams are invisible cost centers. The executive who claims credit for a clean audit without doing the work gets the promotion. The team that actually built the controls gets restructured.
- **Battle**: Jody's counter-rule: visible accountability creates competitive moat. When YOUR team's work is traceable, auditable, and attributed, you can't be replaced by someone who just takes credit. The authorship record of compliance becomes the defense.

## DOB Mapping Template

For each chapter, create a `dob-map.md` with this structure:

```markdown
# Chapter [N]: [Title]

## Desire
[What the reader wants — specific, emotional, drawn from intent]

## Obstacle  
[What stands in the way — honest, weighted, real]

## Battle
[How the chapter fights through — earned, specific, uses author's experience]

## Connection to Throughline
[How this chapter advances the book-level DOB arc]

## Key Author Material Needed
[What Q&A answers or examples are required to make this chapter authentic]
```

## What DOB Is NOT

- Not a formula for three-paragraph essays
- Not an excuse to manufacture fake drama
- Not a structure that resolves neatly — the best DOB arcs leave the reader mid-battle with a decision
- Not applied at the individual rule, tip, or section level — always chapter-level or piece-level minimum
