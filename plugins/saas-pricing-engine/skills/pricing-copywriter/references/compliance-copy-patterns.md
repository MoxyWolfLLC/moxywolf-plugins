# Compliance & Defense Vertical Copy Patterns

Language, tone, and messaging patterns for defense contractors, government agencies, and compliance-focused buyers.

## Tone Guidelines

### Use This Voice
- Precise, confident, professional
- Evidence-based claims with specific numbers
- Industry terminology used correctly (not buzzwordy)
- Understated competence — let results speak

### Avoid
- "Supercharge your compliance" (too casual)
- "Game-changing" / "Revolutionary" (overused, underdelivers)
- "Simple" / "Easy" (compliance is inherently complex; don't minimize it)
- Exclamation marks (one per page maximum, if at all)
- Emoji in any customer-facing pricing context

### Examples

**Instead of**: "Our awesome compliance platform makes STIG checking a breeze!"
**Write**: "Reduce STIG assessment time by 60% with automated checklist evaluation and continuous monitoring."

**Instead of**: "Enterprise-grade security for everyone!"
**Write**: "Built for environments where compliance isn't optional. SOC 2 Type II certified. FedRAMP authorized."

## Pricing-Specific Language for Gov/Defense

### Plan Names
Standard SaaS plan names (Starter/Pro/Enterprise) work, but consider:
- **Individual** / **Team** / **Organization** / **Enterprise** (more formal)
- **Practitioner** / **Department** / **Agency** (gov-aligned)
- Avoid "Basic" (implies inadequacy) and "Premium" (vague)

### Price Communication
- Always show annual pricing prominently (gov buyers think annually)
- Show GSA pricing separately if on a schedule
- Include "volume licensing available" for 25+ seats
- "Contact us for government pricing" as a prominent link
- Never hide pricing behind "Contact Sales" unless enterprise — gov buyers want to see if you're in their budget range before engaging procurement

### Value Propositions That Resonate

For **Compliance Officers / ISSMs:**
- "Maintain continuous compliance across [X] STIG benchmarks"
- "Reduce assessment preparation time from weeks to hours"
- "Generate audit-ready documentation automatically"
- "Map controls across NIST 800-53, CMMC, and STIG frameworks simultaneously"

For **Defense Contractors:**
- "Meet CMMC Level [X] requirements with automated evidence collection"
- "Prepare for DIBCAC assessments with confidence"
- "Track POA&M items with automated remediation guidance"

For **IT Directors / CISOs:**
- "Visibility across your entire compliance posture in a single dashboard"
- "Reduce the risk of audit findings with continuous monitoring"
- "Demonstrate compliance to auditors in minutes, not months"

For **Procurement Officers:**
- "Available on GSA Schedule [number]"
- "SEWP / CIO-SP3 compatible"
- "Annual subscription with NET-30/60 terms"
- "No per-transaction fees — predictable annual cost"

### Trust Signals Hierarchy

For defense/gov buyers, prioritize trust signals in this order:
1. **Authorization status** (FedRAMP, IL-level, ATO)
2. **Customer logos** (DoD agencies, prime contractors)
3. **Compliance certifications** (SOC 2, ISO 27001)
4. **Uptime/SLA metrics** (historical performance)
5. **Testimonials** (from titled compliance professionals)
6. **Case studies** (with quantified outcomes)
7. **Team credentials** (former gov, cleared personnel)

### Procurement-Friendly CTAs

Instead of typical SaaS CTAs, offer:
- "Request pricing sheet" (formal, gov-friendly)
- "Schedule a compliance briefing" (adds value beyond a sales pitch)
- "Download capability statement" (standard gov procurement document)
- "View contract vehicles" (for gov procurement officers)
- "Start a free evaluation" (not "trial" — evaluation sounds more rigorous)
